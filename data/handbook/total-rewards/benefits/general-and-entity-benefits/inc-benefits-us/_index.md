**Clear benefits, real support**

## Getting Started with Your Benefits

You have **30 days from your start date** to sign up for benefits through PlanSource (our benefits system).

> **Important:** If you don't choose a plan within 30 days, you'll automatically get the [**Medical Waiver Allowance**](/handbook/total-rewards/benefits/general-and-entity-benefits/inc-benefits-us/#medical-waiver-allowance) and won't have other optional coverage.

For help understanding how benefits work, check out the [PlanSource Video Library](https://videolibrary.plansource.com/). You can also find payroll deduction details on our [Accounting and Reporting page](/handbook/finance/accounting/).

### Who Can Get Benefits?

You're eligible if you're a full-time GitLab team member working at least 30 hours per week. Your benefits start on your first day.

**You can also cover:**

- Your spouse or domestic partner
- Your children up to age 26 (including adopted and stepchildren)
- Adult children with disabilities who can't support themselves

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Important Notes & Domestic Partner Details</summary>

- If you and an eligible family member both work at GitLab, you can only be covered once under our plans
- For domestic partners: You'll need to complete a [Domestic Partner Affidavit](https://drive.google.com/file/d/11cV-WXJXu8G626muav2aJvpYS4CMleeE/view?usp=sharing) (except during new hire or open enrollment). Domestic partner coverage may be taxable income and premium contributions may not be pre-tax eligible under Section 125

## Medical Coverage

GitLab provides medical insurance through Cigna nationwide, with Kaiser options available for California and Hawaii residents. If you already have coverage elsewhere, you can opt out and receive a monthly allowance instead.

**Need Help Choosing?** Use the [Flimp PlanSelect tool](https://myplanselect.com/navigation/Gitlab/2025) to find the right plan for you, or contact Cigna One Guide Service for personalized assistance walking through your options.

### Medical Waiver Allowance

Already have group medical coverage? You can opt out and receive a **$150** *(in 2025 $300)* monthly benefit allowance while still enrolling in dental, vision, and other benefits. You must actively waive medical coverage in PlanSource to receive this allowance. *If you do not enroll in a plan within your benefits election period, you will automatically be enrolled in the Medical Waiver Allowance.*

<details markdown="1">
<summary>How to apply for Short-Term Disability</summary>

If you're unable to work due to a qualifying disability, contact the Absence Management team at [leaves@gitlab.com](mailto:leaves@gitlab.com) as soon as possible. See [Sick Time reporting procedures](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/time-off-types/#sick-time)
**The process:**

1. The Absence Management team will guide you through the application process. This will include:
   a. Providing you with filing options (online portal, phone, or paper)
   b. Providing NYLife with the employer eligibility intake information
   c. Sending you the Assignment of Benefits form

2. After 26 weeks (maximum benefit period), you'll need to determine if you can return to work

**Returning to work:** Email leaves@gitlab.com a physician's Return to Work authorization at least 5 calendar days before your planned return date. Include any needed accommodations.

**If you can't return after 26 weeks:** You'll move to unpaid leave with the option to continue benefits through COBRA. You can also apply for long-term disability at this time.

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>NYLife Claim Process for Short-Term Disability</summary>

**Step 1: Submit Your [Short-Term Disability](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1guydUTEc0vBFMaa_IsSktZ5hXAbOXdvD/view?usp=sharing) Application.** Complete the claimant portion and have your physician finish the rest. You can either:

- Send directly to NYLife (using the mail/fax number on the form), or
- Return to leaves@gitlab.com who will submit it for you

Let the Absence Management team know which option you choose.

**Step 2: NYLife Review Process**

- Within 3 business days, NYLife will contact you if they need additional information
- They may also contact your physician (with your authorization) and verify details with Absence Management
- Review begins immediately once they have your information

**Step 3: Decision**

- **If denied:** NYLife will call to explain the decision, and Absence Management will process any owed pay
- **If approved:** Both you and Absence Management receive confirmation with your approval date and estimated return-to-work date

**Step 4: Final Processing** Absence Management files all paperwork in Workday and processes the final decision.

</details>

### Flexible Spending & Health Savings Accounts

Tax-advantaged accounts to help manage healthcare and dependent care costs:

**Health Savings Account (HSA)**

GitLab contributes to your HSA each month: $100 for individual coverage and $125 for family coverage (team member + dependents). You must be enrolled in the High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) to be eligible for HSA contributions and enrollment. Please see the [**US Benefits Guide**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/15L8LMWI1CoUUoU3DcimxU3edITKs8QrV/view) for current year contribution limits.

<details markdown="1">
<summary>HSA Details</summary>

Your HSA lets you set aside pre-tax money for qualified medical expenses, reducing your overall healthcare costs.

**Key benefits:**

- **Tax advantages:** Contributions reduce taxable income, earnings grow tax-free, and withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free
- **Flexible timing:** Money rolls over annually and never expires—it's yours forever
- **Investment options:** Once your balance reaches the minimum, you can invest in mutual funds, stocks, and bonds
- **Future flexibility:** At age 65, withdraw for any purpose without penalty (medical expenses remain tax-free)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>HSA Account Rollover & Administration</summary>

**Transferring from a previous HSA:** Contact WEX (Discovery) at 866.451.3399 or customerservice@discoverybenefits.com to request a transfer form. Complete with your old provider's account information, WEX will handle the rest.

**If you leave GitLab:** Your HSA stays with you forever. You'll become responsible for the monthly administration fee once GitLab is no longer your employer.

**Domestic Partner Coverage:** If you're not legally married, your domestic partner's expenses aren't HSA-eligible. However, if they're covered under your family HDHP, they can open their own HSA. Both of you can contribute up to the full family maximum to your respective HSAs.

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Mid-Year HSA Contribution Change</summary>

You can adjust your HSA contributions anytime throughout the year. Changes take effect on the 1st of the following month. Use the Qualifying Life Event Process and select "Mid-Year HSA Contribution."

**Front-loading contributions:** Contact WEX directly to front-load your portion of HSA contributions. Remember to:

- Include GitLab's contribution in your calculation
- Update your payroll deductions in PlanSource accordingly
- Track your own maximum contribution limits (GitLab can't monitor contributions made outside payroll)

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Health Savings Account and Offboarding GitLab</summary>

Your HSA and all funds are yours to keep when you leave GitLab. You can continue using the money for eligible healthcare expenses at your next employer or let it grow over time.

WEX may charge management fees for direct account administration. Contact WEX (Discovery) at 866.451.3399, via live chat, or customerservice@discoverybenefits.com with questions.

</details>

**Flexible Spending Accounts**

FSAs let you pay for healthcare and dependent care expenses with pre-tax dollars. Set aside money from each paycheck based on your estimated annual expenses. Changes only allowed during open enrollment or qualifying life events. Please see the [**US Benefits Guide**](https://drive.google.com/file/d/15L8LMWI1CoUUoU3DcimxU3edITKs8QrV/view) for current year contribution limits.

1. **Dependent Care FSA** Covers childcare and eldercare expenses. Qualifying dependents: children under 13, disabled spouse, or parents needing care. Claims incurred through March 15, submitted by March 31.

2. **Medical FSA** Covers medical, dental, vision, and over-the-counter medications. Cannot participate if you have an HSA.

3. **Limited Purpose FSA** Covers dental and vision only. Available if you have an HSA.

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Rollover Details for Medical and Limited Purpose FSA</summary>

Rollover funds from prior year won't be available until 5-15 business days after March 31 of the current tax year (no later than April 22).

**Contribution and Rollover Limits:**

- **2025:** $3,300 max contribution, $660 max rollover
- **2026:** $3,400 max contribution, $680 max rollover

**Important Notes:**

- Minimum $5 required for rollover; amounts $5 or less are forfeited
- You can use rollover funds the following year, even if you don't enroll in FSA again

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Flexible Spending Accounts and Leaving GitLab</summary>

**Medical FSA:** Your account terminates on your last day unless you continue through COBRA. You can use your full FSA balance up to and including your last day.

- **COBRA option:** Available if you've contributed more than you've spent
- **COBRA payments:** Continue monthly contributions on post-tax basis
- **Claims deadline:** Submit all claims within 90 days of termination

**Dependent Care FSA:** Cannot be continued through COBRA. Use funds for expenses through your last day only.

- **Claims deadline:** Submit all claims within 90 days of termination

**Questions?** Contact WEX (Discovery) at 866.451.3399 or customerservice@discoverybenefits.com

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>FSA Period to Submit Claims</summary>

You have 90 days after the plan year ends to submit claims (known as the runout period).

</details>

<details markdown="1">
<summary>Mid-Year FSA Contribution Changes</summary>

You can only increase FSA contributions during a Qualifying Life Event; reductions aren't allowed. Additional funds can only be used for expenses incurred on or after your qualifying event date.

**Example:** If you originally elected $500 and increased it to $2,000 after getting married, you can only be reimbursed up to $500 for expenses before your marriage date. The additional $1,500 is available for expenses after your marriage.

</details>
