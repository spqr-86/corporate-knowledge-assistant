<details markdown="1"> <summary>Denmark Statutory Leave Policies</summary>

Below is the information related to Denmark's statutory leave policies. Please note that team members hired in Denmark are hired via GX (PEO) and you should always check with GX first for the most up to date information.

**Denmark Holiday Leave**

- All full-time workers are entitled to 5 weeks (25 days) paid holiday leave a year under the Holiday Act (Lov om ferie).
- The holiday year runs from 1 September to 31 August, and any accrued holidays can be taken until 31 December of the following year, allowing a total period of 16 months to utilise the leave.

**Denmark Pregnancy, Maternity and Paternity Leave**

In total, parents in Denmark get 52 weeks of paid parental leave. The leave entitlement is structured as follows:

**Maternity Leave: For Mothers**

- 4 weeks of pregnancy leave: Can be taken up to 4 weeks before the due date, with maternity/paternity benefits.
- 2 weeks after birth: Must be taken immediately after birth and cannot be transferred to the other parent.
- 8 weeks at birth: Must be taken within the first 10 weeks after the child is born; can be extended if returning to part-time work. This leave can be transferred to the other parent if they meet the conditions for receiving maternity/paternity benefits.
- 9 weeks of leave: Cannot be transferred; must be taken within 1 year of the child's birth unless prevented by special circumstances.
- 5 weeks of leave: Must be taken within 1 year, but can be extended or postponed until the child reaches age 9 if conditions are met. This leave can be transferred to the other parent if they meet the conditions for receiving maternity/paternity benefits.

**Total for mothers**: 4 weeks pregnancy + 24 weeks maternity leave (with 13 weeks transferable under certain conditions)

**Paternity Leave: For Fathers or Co-Mothers**

- 2 weeks at birth: Must be taken within the first 2 weeks after birth, and can be taken flexibly over the first 10 weeks with employer agreement. The 2 weeks of leave at birth can be taken as individual days or split over the 10-week period. The leave corresponds to the employee's weekly working hours (e.g., full-time 37 hours/week = 74 hours total for the 2 weeks).
- 9 weeks of leave: Cannot be transferred; must be taken within 1 year unless special circumstances prevent it.
- 13 weeks of leave: Must be taken within 1 year, but can be extended or postponed until the child reaches age 9 if conditions are met. This leave can be transferred to the other parent if conditions for maternity/paternity benefits are met at the time of the leave.

**Total for fathers/co-mothers**: 24 weeks paternity leave (with 13 weeks transferable under certain conditions)

**Maternity and Paternity Leave Payment Eligibility**

As a team member, you can receive maternity or paternity benefits if you meet these three conditions:

- You must be employed on the first day of your leave or the day before.
- You must have at least 160 hours within the last four full months before the leave.
- You must have at least 40 hours per month for at least three of the four months.
- In addition, you must also be with your child daily, i.e., physically spend time with the child.

Payment Denmark automatically receives the information about your employment and hours from your employer.

**Maternity and Paternity Leave Payment**
The maximum maternity and paternity leave payment in Denmark is **DKK 4,865 per week (2025)** for full-time employees working 37 hours per week.

- If you are employed full-time and earn more than DKK 21,092 per month, you will receive the maximum weekly benefit of DKK 4,865 before tax.
- Maternity and paternity benefits are calculated based on your hourly wage (excluding Labour Market Contribution) and the number of hours of leave taken per week.
- In 2025, the maximum hourly rate for maternity benefits is DKK 131.49 per hour (4,865 ÷ 37 hours). If your hourly wage is lower than this, benefits will be paid at your usual hourly rate.
- Employees with a permanent job in addition to their education can apply for maternity benefits as a salaried employee for the hours during which they work.

If eligible, [GitLab's Parental leave](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/leave-types/#gitlab-parental-leave) will be applied concurrently with the statutory leave and/or pay entitlements the team member is eligible for. GitLab will top up state maternity/paternity benefits to ensure you receive 100% of your salary for up to 16 weeks (*). Any additional leave taken (up to 52 weeks total) will be unpaid by GitLab and paid at the statutory rate only.

*GX (GitLab’s employer of record) will seek reimbursement from the public authority; Udbetaling Danmark for the state funded portion.

**Applying for Maternity and Paternity Leave Benefits in Denmark**

The application process for maternity and paternity benefits is the same for both parents.

1. Notify Your Employer (GitLab & GX) that you will be taking leave. Your employer must notify Udbetaling Danmark via virk.dk/barselsdagpenge on your first day of leave or later. Enter your Parental Leave into Workday by selecting the “GitLab Parental Leave” option under the Leaves dropdown menu. This request will route to the Absence Management team who will contact you with more information.
2. If you are eligible for [GitLab's Parental leave](/handbook/people-group/time-off-and-absence/leave-types/#gitlab-parental-leave): If you receive pay during the leave, reimbursement will be sought by your employer. You can only apply for maternity/paternity benefit yourself when the salary stops. Your employer must notify Udbetaling Danmark when the salary stops. They can do this at the earliest on your first day without pay. As soon as your employer has given notice, you will automatically receive a letter via Digital Post with access to apply for maternity/paternity benefits.
3. If you are not eligible for GitLab’s Parental Leave: You must apply for maternity/paternity benefit yourself from the start of the leave. Your employer or unemployment insurance fund must first notify Udbetaling Danmark that you are going on leave. They can do this your first day on leave at the earliest. As soon as they have given notice, you will receive a letter via Digital Post with a link to apply for maternity/paternity benefits. It also says when you last applied.

Application Deadlines: You must apply for maternity/paternity benefits no later than:

- 8 weeks after childbirth (if not receiving pay)
- 8 weeks after your salary stops (if receiving partial pay)
- 8 weeks after your leave starts (e.g., unpaid paternity leave)
For detailed information regarding statutory/maternity benefits, review the Danish government [website](https://lifeindenmark.borger.dk/family-and-children/family-benefits/maternity-paternity-benefits?frm1=micro-articles-list-e4b24037-c8b4-4132-b97b-90e39d1d895b&frm2=micro-articles-list-a9dba0b7-ac1d-4f9f-9911-c66cf0eedb45).

**Denmark Other Leave Policies**

- **Adoption Leave**: Adoptive parents are entitled to 4 weeks of leave prior to receiving the child, if the adoptive parents have to reside outside of Denmark to adopt. If the child is adopted in Denmark, and it is necessary to reside where the place child is situation, parents are eligible for one week of leave before the adoption. In all cases of adoption, one of the adoptive parents is entitled to adoption leave of 14 weeks and the other with to leave with statutory benefits for 2 weeks. The remaining 12 weeks can be split between the adoptive parents.
- **Care leave(omsorgsdage)**: Intended to allow team members to care for a sick child. This is not a statutory requirement, but is common.
